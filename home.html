An Ad for Myself

Written 12/20/19

Wow, my first click bait (see the last line of First Post if you don’t get it), I won’t do that again, promise. To answer the question, why invest so much time in making a personal website from scratch? There is a balance between doing hard work and gaining value from that labor. I believe I tend to spend more time on the former, and I am repeatedly disappointed when I feel that my efforts aren’t rewarded. This is an investment in harvesting some value that I think I have should be seeing.

A more concrete example: after somebody’s done all the work to make an impact in a research field (via some novel method or results), the delivery of the work ( often as a publication of some kind) needs done well enough for people to notice the importance of the results. Often we estimate the process of publishing work to be negligible compared to the time it takes to do the work worth publishing, but public credit cannot exist without reporting work.

Really, the fruits of some work/effort being advertised here is myself: accept me to your programs/hire me to your professional role, I bet the competition isn’t as foolishly honest nor as mediocre at HTML.
